Frequently Asked Questions
FAQ

All EMS courses require a minimum grade of 75% to progress in the program. Students who fall below this threshold are subject to the remediation policy. A grade of 75% or higher must be maintained to remain enrolled and advance to the next course in the sequence.

No. EMS courses at CAMP Community College require authorization from the EMS Program Director. You must meet with Dr. Cajares before you can be enrolled in any EMS course. Contact the Program Office to schedule your advising appointment.

G3 (Get Skilled, Get a Job, Give Back) is a Virginia statewide tuition assistance program for community college students enrolled in high-demand workforce programs — including EMS. Eligible students may receive assistance covering the cost of tuition after other financial aid is applied. Contact Financial Aid or the EMS Program Office to determine your eligibility.

The Committee on Accreditation of Educational Programs for the EMS Professions (CoAEMSP) issues Letters of Review as a step toward full CAAHEP accreditation. CAMP Community College holds LORs for both AEMT and Paramedic programs — the only Virginia EMS program with this distinction. Graduates of LOR-approved programs are eligible to sit for NREMT certification exams and state licensure, and employers recognize the quality standards this represents.

Yes! Students who hold a current NREMT or Virginia AEMT certification receive 11 credits and advanced placement directly into the Paramedic program. This is a significant benefit that recognizes your prior learning and experience. Contact the Program Director to initiate the advanced standing process.

Clinical placements include regional EMS agencies and hospital partners throughout the Hampton Roads area, including Suffolk Fire & Rescue, Sentara Obici Hospital, and other partner agencies. ALS transport agencies, emergency departments, and specialty units (ICU, L&D, pediatrics) are used for Paramedic-level clinical hours. All sites are coordinated through the Clinical Coordinator under the direction of the Program Director.

Required immunizations include: Hepatitis B series (3-dose), MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella), Varicella (Chickenpox), Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis), annual influenza vaccine, and a current TB screening (2-step PPD or IGRA). Documentation of all immunizations must be submitted before clinical placement. Students should contact their healthcare provider or the local health department to obtain any missing vaccines.

After completing all program requirements and receiving a program completion letter from the Program Director, you can apply for the NREMT certification exam at nremt.org. The Program Director submits your completion status to NREMT, after which you will receive authorization to test (ATT). You then schedule your exam at a Pearson VUE testing center. After passing, you apply to VAOEMS for your Virginia state certification.

Current EMS professionals may receive academic credit for existing certifications and experience through CAMP's Credit for Prior Learning policy. This may include credits awarded for active NREMT certifications, documented field experience, or completed EMS training programs. Contact the Program Director and Registrar's office for specific CPL evaluation procedures.

Yes. Beyond FAFSA, students may qualify for: G3 Tuition Assistance (workforce program aid), workforce development grants, agency-sponsored training (some EMS agencies pay tuition for employees/volunteers), and scholarship programs. Contact Financial Aid and Career Services to explore all options. Some students are also sponsored through agency partnership contracts (e.g., the Norfolk Fire-Rescue AEMT-to-Paramedic program).
